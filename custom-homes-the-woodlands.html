The Woodlands, Texas

Building in
Houston's Premier
Master-Planned Community

Founded in 1974 by visionary developer George P. Mitchell, The Woodlands spans more than 28,000 acres and is home to approximately 120,000 residents. The community officially incorporated as a city in 2021, cementing decades of growth into one of Texas's most desirable addresses. Its reputation for blending natural beauty with world-class amenities continues to attract families, executives, and discerning homeowners from across the country.

The Woodlands is organized into distinct villages, each with its own character: Carlton Woods stands as the pinnacle of luxury living, Sterling Ridge offers wooded privacy and mature landscaping, Creekside Park features modern floorplans on the community's western edge, and Indian Springs and Panther Creek provide established canopies with generous lot sizes. The lifestyle is anchored by the Waterway, Market Street, and Hughes Landing, along with over 220 miles of hike-and-bike trails and championship golf. Schools are served by Conroe ISD, including nationally recognized campuses. For anyone planning a custom build here, our Houston construction cost guide provides a detailed breakdown of what to budget.

Building in The Woodlands

What to Expect
When You Build
in The Woodlands

The Woodlands Development Standards Committee (DSC) reviews every new build before construction begins. Exterior materials, roof pitch, color palettes, and landscaping must all be submitted and approved. Luxury villages like Carlton Woods impose additional requirements, including minimum square footage thresholds and strict architectural style guidelines.

Wooded lots come with aggressive tree preservation requirements, and arborist coordination is standard on virtually every project. The Woodlands sits on sandy loam rather than the heavy clay found in inner Houston, creating different foundation and drainage considerations around Spring Creek and Panther Creek. The community spans both Montgomery and Harris counties, each with its own building codes and inspection processes.
