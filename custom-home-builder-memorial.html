Memorial Custom Homes

Where Heritage
Meets Modern
Craftsmanship

Memorial is defined by its mature tree canopies, generous lot sizes, and a quiet sense of permanence that few Houston neighborhoods can match. Building here demands a builder who respects that character while delivering the performance and finishes today's discerning homeowners expect.

Solanji Construction has been building luxury custom homes throughout Memorial's most distinguished enclaves — including Piney Point Village, Bunker Hill Village, Hunters Creek, and the broader Memorial Villages corridor. We understand the deed restrictions, architectural review processes, and site conditions unique to each community, and we navigate them with the confidence that comes from decades of local experience.

Many of our Memorial projects involve acquiring an existing lot, removing the original structure, and constructing a new residence that takes full advantage of the property's dimensions and orientation. Whether your vision calls for a classic Georgian estate on a half-acre Piney Point lot or a contemporary residence along the Buffalo Bayou corridor, we manage the entire process — permitting, demolition, construction, and final landscaping — under one roof and one point of accountability.

Our commitment to Memorial extends beyond construction. We build homes designed to serve multi-generational families, with thoughtful floor plans that accommodate growing children, visiting grandparents, and the rhythms of daily life. Explore our Houston custom home building capabilities, or review our cost guide to understand what a Memorial build typically involves.

Common Questions

Building in Memorial FAQ

What is the process for building a custom home in Memorial?
The process begins with a lot evaluation and consultation to understand your goals and budget. From there, we coordinate architectural design, navigate the applicable deed restrictions and architectural review committee, secure permits, and manage construction from foundation to final finishes. Most Memorial custom homes take 12 to 18 months to build, depending on size and complexity.
How do deed restrictions work in the Memorial Villages?
Each Memorial Village — Piney Point, Bunker Hill, Hunters Creek, Hedwig Village, Hilshire Village, and Spring Valley — maintains its own set of deed restrictions governing setbacks, building height, lot coverage, exterior materials, and architectural style. Most require architectural review board approval before construction begins. We are well-versed in these requirements and factor them into the design process from day one.
What is the typical home size for a custom build in Memorial?
Custom homes in Memorial generally range from 4,000 to 8,000 square feet, though properties in Piney Point Village and along larger lots can exceed 10,000 square feet. The generous lot sizes in Memorial — often a half acre or more — allow for substantial homes with significant outdoor living space.
How much does it cost to build a custom home in Memorial?
Memorial custom homes typically range from $300 to $500+ per square foot depending on finishes, architectural complexity, and lot preparation requirements. A 5,000-square-foot home with luxury finishes generally falls between $1.5M and $2.5M or more. Visit our cost to build a home in Houston guide for detailed breakdowns.
How long does it take to build in Memorial?
Most Memorial custom homes require 12 to 18 months of construction time, with an additional 2 to 4 months for design, permitting, and architectural review approval. Teardown-and-rebuild projects may add 4 to 6 weeks for demolition and site preparation. We provide a detailed milestone schedule before breaking ground.
Can you tear down an existing home and rebuild on the same lot?
Yes — teardown-and-rebuild projects are one of our specialties in Memorial. Many Memorial lots contain homes from the 1960s and 1970s that no longer meet modern standards. We handle the complete process: demolition, site clearing, new construction, and landscape restoration. This approach allows you to stay in the neighborhood you love while getting a home built to today's standards.
